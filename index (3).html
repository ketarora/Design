The idea

You learn to think, plan, and make a video before you hit generate.

Most people treat AI as a magic button: prompt → video → done. This lab is the opposite. You build the same 3 videos bit-by-bit across the week — so you see how your thinking evolves and where the real craft lives.

By the end you'll have 3 solid videos you can publish anywhere, not just a folder of random clips.

What happens each day

7 sessions total
Day 01
Script as a video plan
We write scripts for all 3 videos, built so they can directly become AI-video prompts. You learn to structure your idea so the AI actually understands what you want.
Day 02
Image from simple action
For an 8-second video, we break it into frames and generate still images for each "simple action" using a clear visual language. No guessing — just frames you can actually animate.
Day 03
Dress the image
You learn to describe each image deeply: first frame and last frame, in colour, lighting, composition, and mood. You'll write prompts that feel cinematic, not generic.
Day 04
Moving between clips
We design the transitions between shots — how one clip flows into the next. This is where your video starts to feel like a real story, not a slideshow.
Day 05
Time to generate
You mix all the pieces and generate the full videos from your refined prompts. We watch, compare, and tweak until it feels like your style.
Day 06
Sound and life
You craft audio or voice from the same scripts so your 3 videos feel complete: visuals + sound, not just silent clips.
Day 07
Live session + feedback
On Sunday, we do a live interactive session for questions, feedback, and how your videos actually land when shared. Think of it as "video-making office hours."

Non-linear

You don't make one video a day. You build the same 3 videos bit-by-bit across the week, so you see how your thinking evolves over time.

Hands-on

Every day you make something real — not just watch. You write, frame, describe, transition, sound-design. A 2-hour live session alone won't change how you work.

Iterative

You compare your early attempts with your final versions, so you see where the magic is actually happening. I make test videos alongside you.

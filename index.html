{ 02 } — THE SCHEDULE

Here's what happens
in 6 days + 1 Sunday.

01 Day 1

Script as a Video Plan

We write scripts for all 3 videos, built so they can directly become AI-video prompts. You'll learn how to structure your idea so the AI actually understands what you want.

02 Day 2

Image from Simple Action

For an 8-second video, we break it into frames and generate still images for each "simple action", using a clear visual language. No guessing, just frames you can actually animate.

03 Day 3

Dress the Image

You learn how to describe each image deeply: first frame and last frame, in colour, lighting, composition, and mood. You'll practice writing prompts that feel cinematic, not generic.

04 Day 4

Moving Between Clips

We design the transitions between shots: how one clip flows into the next. This is where your video starts to feel like a real story, not a slideshow.

05 Day 5

Time to Generate Video

You mix all the pieces and generate the full videos from your refined prompts. We watch, compare, and tweak until it feels like your style.

06 Day 6

Sound and Life

You craft audio or voice from the same scripts so your 3 videos feel complete: visuals + sound, not just silent clips.

07 Sunday Live

Live Session + Feedback

On Sunday, we do a live interactive session for questions, feedback, and how your videos actually land when shared. Think of it as "video-making office hours".

{ 03 } — WHY THIS WORKS

A 2-hour live session alone
won't change how you work.

What changes your craft is doing it yourself, then talking about it.

Non-Linear

You don't just make one video a day. Instead, you build the same 3 videos bit-by-bit across the week, so you see how your thinking evolves.

Hands-On

Every day you make something real, not just watch. You write, you frame, you describe, you transition, you sound-design.

Iterative

You can compare your early attempts with your final versions, so you see where the magic is really happening.

You're not just "watching another YouTube video" and moving on. You're building your own process, and I'll be making short test videos alongside you, so we can compare what works and what doesn't.
